- **Hong Kong** — SFC framework for STOs. Licensed exchanges (OSL, HashKey).
- **Japan** — Revised FIEA allows security tokens. SBI Holdings, Nomura active.

Land and real estate represent ~$330T globally. Tokenized real estate is less than 0.01% penetrated. Land tokenization specifically has almost no dedicated platforms — this is your blue ocean.

## 6. Regulatory Landscape

> **Every tokenized real asset is almost certainly a security. Do not argue otherwise. Structure as compliant securities from day one.**

### Key Exemptions

**Reg D 506(b):**
- Unlimited raise, up to 35 non-accredited + unlimited accredited
- NO general solicitation
- File Form D
- Best for raising from existing relationships

**Reg D 506(c) [RECOMMENDED as primary exemption]:**
- Unlimited raise, accredited ONLY (must verify)
- General solicitation IS allowed (can market publicly)
- File Form D
- Best for tokenization platforms because you can publicly market offerings

**Reg S:**
- Non-US persons outside the US
- Combine with Reg D (domestic + international)
- Selling restrictions required

**Reg A+ (Tier 2):**
- Up to $75M/year
- Open to non-accredited
- SEC qualification required ($100K-$300K legal/audit, 3-6 months)
- Freely tradable
- Consider for Phase 2

**Reg CF:**
- Up to $5M/year
- All investors
- Must use registered portal (Republic, Wefunder)
- Good for community-focused offerings

### Token Structure

```
[Real Asset] → [SPV/LLC] → [Operating Agreement] → [XRPL Tokens = Membership Interests]
```

- SPV/LLC holds asset (liability isolation)
- Operating Agreement defines rights, distributions, voting, transferability
- XRPL tokens represent LLC membership interests
- Transfer restrictions enforced on-chain (authorized trust lines) and off-chain (KYC verification)

### KYC/AML

- Likely need MSB registration with FinCEN (or partner with registered entity)
- KYC on all investors: identity verification, accredited verification (for 506(c))
- AML screening: OFAC SDN list, PEP lists, sanctions
- Providers: Jumio, Sumsub, Parallel Markets, VerifyInvestor.com, Chainalysis

### State Considerations

- Reg D 506(b/c) preempts state registration, but file Form D notice with each state where investors reside
- **Wyoming:** Most crypto-friendly. DAO LLC law, digital asset custody framework. Consider for SPV incorporation.
- **Delaware:** Standard for LLC formation. Well-understood law.

### Legal Partners Needed

1. Securities attorney ($30K-$75K per offering)
2. Blockchain/crypto counsel ($15K-$30K platform-level)
3. Real estate attorney ($5K-$15K per asset)
4. Tax advisor ($10K-$20K initially)

| **XRPL** | **$0.0002-$0.001** | **$2.40-$12** |

XRPL is 10,000x cheaper than Ethereum for distribution-heavy RWA operations.

### Key XRPL Features for RWA

1. **Built-in DEX** — Native order book. Tokenized assets tradable without smart contracts. Unique among major chains.
2. **Trust Lines** — Opt-in to hold tokens. Natural compliance checkpoint (require KYC before enabling).
3. **Authorized Trust Lines (Require Auth)** — Only verified investors can hold tokens. On-chain whitelist.
4. **Global Freeze** — Emergency freeze capability. Required for regulated securities. Gives regulators comfort.
5. **Clawback** — Court-ordered recovery capability. Critical for regulated securities.
6. **No Smart Contract Risk** — Tokens are native protocol objects. No audit needed. No reentrancy/overflow bugs. Battle-tested since 2012.
7. **3-5 Second Finality** — Deterministic, no congestion concerns.
8. **Native Escrow** — Time-locked or conditional transfers for vesting/milestones.
9. **XLS-20 NFTs** — Represent unique assets (individual parcels, deeds) at ~$0.0002/mint.
10. **RLUSD Stablecoin** — Ripple's own stablecoin on XRPL for distribution settlement.
